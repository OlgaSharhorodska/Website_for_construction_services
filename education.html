10 Reasons to Use Masonry

Durability

Masonry is extremely resilient. The inherent strength and rigidity of masonry structures can provide increased stability and resistance to natural disasters such as earthquakes, hurricanes and tornadoes. Brick, stone and concrete block are naturally durable and resistant to “wear and tear”. These materials do not degrade easily when exposed to wind, rain or temperature fluctuations and therefore maintain their strength and appearance over many years.

Fire Resistance

Concrete block, stone and clay products such as brick and terra cotta are all non- combustible. A building constructed out of masonry offers the following safety benefits during a fire:

  • Maintain the structural integrity of load bearing walls
  • Protect building egress
  • Prevent fire-spread within a building and to adjacent buildings
  • Prevent emission of toxic gasses or smoke
  • Provide firefighter protection

In the event the sprinkler system fails to properly operate during a fire, the built-in fire resistive properties of masonry walls will contain the fire until it can be controlled while allowing precious additional time for building occupants to exit.

Energy Savings

Thermal mass is the ability of a material to absorb, store and release heat. Masonry products naturally possess high thermal mass which helps moderate temperature swings and prevents energy loss, directly lowering heating and cooling bills. A building constructed out of concrete blocks for example will hold heat longer in cool weather and cooled air inside for longer periods in hot weather long after the heat or air conditioning has been turned off. This quality along with airtight walls will keep the heat energy where you want it whether it be inside or outside of the building.

Structural Integrity

Masonry construction might be best known for its strong structural integrity, which is a result of the careful combination of materials, design principles and construction techniques. Masonry materials have excellent compressive strength and load-bearing capabilities making them highly suitable for constructing walls, columns, arches and other structural elements that can support significant loads over time. The weight of a masonry structure is distributed downward through a collective of individual masonry units and mortar joints.

Aesthetic Appeal

Masonry Construction offers a seemingly limitless amount of design possibilities. Masonry can be adapted to various architectural styles and designs making it versatile for both traditional and modern buildings. Different varieties of brick, stone, precast concrete and concrete masonry units can be selected to create visually appealing textures, patterns and colors enhancing the overall aesthetics of a structure.

Low Maintenance

If a masonry building is designed by knowledgeable architects and constructed with skilled masons the higher initial build costs will be offset by lower maintenance costs over the life of the building. Masonry materials are resistant to severe weather, corrosion, rot, decay, UV radiation and pests like termites and carpenter bees. When damage does occur the repairs can often be performed on a localized basis. This can be more cost effective and less disruptive than repairing larger sections of other modes of construction.

Environmentally Sustainable

Many of the masonry materials you might choose are natural and locally available which can reduce the environmental impact associated with transportation over long distances. Masonry buildings can last for centuries and be repurposed for different uses with a creative imagination and a little skillful effort. It is common to see old brick warehouses, breweries, factories and tanneries in the historical districts of our larger cities converted into lofts and apartments. A large assortment of masonry products are manufactured out of recycled content and often entire brick buildings are salvaged and the bricks reused for entirely new projects. Furthermore, masonry products typically have no volatile organic compound (VOC) emissions. Most don’t contain integral organic-based surface coatings, binders or sealants like many other building materials. This contributes to better indoor air quality and a healthier environment for everyone.

Sound Insulation

Masonry materials like concrete block are ideal for providing sound insulation due to their density and mass. A masonry wall can act as a barrier by diffusing incident noise over a wide range of frequencies and it can be an effective sound absorption material for absorbing noise generated within a room. This is particularly useful for buildings located in areas with significant noise pollution or where sound privacy is important.

Historical Significance

Masonry construction has a long history and was used to create many iconic structures. Its use dates back to various ancient civilizations; some include the Egyptians, Greeks and Romans. The Pyramids of Giza, Parthenon and Colosseum are prime examples of the monumental structures that have survived for centuries, showcasing the remarkable craftsmanship and engineering skills of their time. Throughout the Medieval Period masonry was essential for constructing castles, fortifications and cathedrals all across Europe. In America’s early beginnings the use of masonry was integral to the development of colonial settlements and historical buildings. Structures such as Colonial-Era houses, government buildings and town squares often featured masonry components that reflected the prevailing architectural styles of their time. In fact, masonry has shaped the urban landscapes of cities all over the world. Cobblestone streets, brick facades and stone-paved plazas are examples of masonry elements that contribute to the charm and character of historic urban developments. Many monuments and memorials have been constructed using masonry materials to commemorate historical events, wars, individuals and achievements. Today, with many architects and designers incorporating historical masonry elements into contemporary buildings, it is easy to see how masonry from the past continues to influence modern architecture. Incorporating masonry into your project can contribute to the preservation of architectural traditions and cultural heritage.

Increased Property Value

Due to their durability, aesthetics, low maintenance and other benefits, properties with masonry often have higher resale value. Masonry is often associated with a sense of permanence and solidity. These qualities resonate with buyers seeking a stable and comfortable lifestyle. Properties with masonry construction have a track record of holding their value well over time. This history of appreciation can make potential buyers more confident in their investment.
